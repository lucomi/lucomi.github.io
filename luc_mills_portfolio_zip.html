Multi-Platform Data Architecture

The Challenge

SiriusXM's data organization spanned three platforms with no unified governance. Business units consumed overlapping datasets through divergent paths, and the AI/ML team was blocked on feature engineering because the Databricks migration was deprioritized.

What I Did

Mapped every data source, pipeline, and consumer across the estate. Established data contracts between producers and consumers with defined freshness SLAs, quality rules, and escalation paths. Quantified the cost differential between Teradata and Databricks to build the migration business case, and prioritized the Databricks migration over Teradata decommission to unblock AI/ML workloads.

Impact

Unified governance across six stakeholder groups. Identified 10× cost differentials that made the platform consolidation case self-evident. Unblocked AI/ML feature engineering for the recommendation model initiative.

AI Insights — RAG Architecture

The Challenge

GovTribe needed to transform half a billion government procurement records into actionable intelligence. Users wanted to ask questions like "Who are likely bidders for this cybersecurity RFP?" and get instant, accurate answers.

What I Built

Designed and shipped AI Insights — a production RAG system from zero. Chose Pinecone as a managed vector database (small team, couldn't self-manage). Built data enrichment pipelines extracting structured metadata. Implemented quality gates at three layers to ensure the LLM never received stale or incomplete context. Optimized costs by routing queries to GPT-3.5 or GPT-4 based on complexity.

Impact

Grew ARR from $2M to $4.5M. Increased daily active user engagement by 25%. Scaled engineering from 3 to 8. Achieved sub-2 second end-to-end latency and cut API costs 40% through model routing and context compression.
